# Fredericksburg / Gillespie Acquisition Checklist

Reusable baseline for future Fredericksburg and Gillespie County STR and hospitality-oriented acquisitions.

## 1) Jurisdiction decision tree

### Step 1: Where is the parcel?
- Confirm exact parcel location using:
  - City Limit / 1-Mile ETJ map
  - CAD situs/legal
  - listing remarks
  - survey/title if needed

### Step 2: Classify the parcel into one of 3 buckets
1. **Inside Fredericksburg city limits**
2. **Inside Fredericksburg ETJ but outside city limits**
3. **Unincorporated Gillespie County outside both city limits and ETJ**

### Why this matters
This classification determines:
- STR permit burden
- inspection burden
- who gets local HOT
- operating constraints
- what site-development issues matter most

---

## 2) STR permit / inspection decision tree

### A. Inside Fredericksburg city limits
- STR permit required
- annual inspection required
- listing/advertising rules apply
- occupancy/parking/quiet-hours rules apply

### B. Inside Fredericksburg ETJ
- current city guidance says **no city STR permit required**
- still verify parcel is truly ETJ and not city limits
- still owe Fredericksburg HOT + Texas HOT
- main diligence shifts to site/utility/access/use constraints

### C. Unincorporated Gillespie County outside city + ETJ
- no countywide STR permit page found in official county research
- still owe Gillespie County HOT + Texas HOT
- county-level constraints become the main diligence focus

---

## 3) HOT decision tree

### A. Inside Fredericksburg city limits
- 7% Fredericksburg HOT
- 6% Texas HOT
- 13% total guest-facing HOT
- quarterly filing with city

### B. Inside Fredericksburg ETJ
- current city guidance says city HOT still applies in the ETJ
- 7% Fredericksburg HOT
- 6% Texas HOT
- 13% total

### C. Unincorporated Gillespie County outside city + ETJ
- 7% Gillespie County HOT
- 6% Texas HOT
- 13% total

### HOT diligence questions
- Who currently remits HOT?
- Is the account active and in good standing?
- Any delinquency, penalty, or filing gap?
- Is the property actually in city, ETJ, or county-only territory?

---

## 4) Fredericksburg city-limit STR operating checklist

Use only if the parcel is in city limits.

### Permit / compliance
- Verify active STR permit number
- Verify expiration / renewal date
- Verify inspection status
- Check any complaint history if obtainable

### Operations rules baseline
- Occupancy: 2 per bedroom + 2 additional persons, max 12 unless special exception
- Parking: 1 on-site space per bedroom
- Quiet hours: 10pm–7am in residential zones
- Local responsible party required
- Listing must include permit number and operating information

### Physical safety baseline
- smoke alarms
- CO detector where applicable
- fire extinguisher per floor
- legal egress from bedrooms
- pool/spa compliance if applicable

---

## 5) ETJ / county physical diligence checklist

These items matter most for ETJ and unincorporated county deals.

### Septic / OSSF
- Confirm existing septic type and capacity
- Ask whether added bedrooms or second-key use changes septic assumptions
- Verify permits / drawings / inspections if available
- Check whether planned additions or use intensification require OSSF action

### Floodplain / engineering
- Check floodplain map and county engineering review triggers
- Determine whether development/fill/grading/paving/additions trigger county engineering review
- Confirm whether a Development Permit Determination Form is needed

### Access / ROW / driveway
- Confirm legal and practical driveway access
- Verify whether any new or modified access needs county ROW permit
- If TxDOT frontage exists, verify TxDOT driveway permitting implications

### Addressing
- Confirm official address assignment
- If multiple dwellings or rentable structures exist, confirm whether separate structures require separate addressing treatment

### Utilities / infrastructure
- electric service adequacy
- water source / well / utility reliability
- internet quality for STR operations
- HVAC capacity, especially if adding conditioned space

---

## 6) STR underwriting checklist

### Revenue thesis
- Is the property a plain house, a two-door STR, or a compound-style asset?
- Can the suite/casita/add-on truly function as a second rentable key?
- Is the bedroom count being marketed today understating the real post-improvement value?

### Comp hierarchy
1. same street
2. same neighborhood / HOA
3. within 1 mile
4. within 5 miles
5. expand only with justification

### Revenue comp questions
- Are there public comps for:
  - main house + cottage
  - duplex/two-key operation
  - rent-separately-or-together model
- What ADR and occupancy are realistic for:
  - main house
  - guest suite/casita
  - full property booking

### LTR fallback
- What is the long-term rental floor if STR underperforms?
- Does the property fail or survive as LTR?

---

## 7) Appraisal-risk checklist for added bedrooms / enclosed porches

### Before assuming value creation, confirm:
- Will the added area count as true livable square footage?
- Is it permitted or permit-able?
- Is it conditioned?
- Are ceiling height and finish level acceptable?
- Are legal egress requirements met for bedrooms?
- Will appraiser likely treat it as GLA vs enclosed porch/sunroom/non-GLA?

### Questions for any enclosure plan
- Is the space already under roof?
- Is the floor already suitable to remain?
- How many new exterior walls are actually needed?
- Will mini-split sizing support the added space?
- Does the enclosure improve ADR enough to justify capex?
- Does it hurt porch charm or overall guest experience?

---

## 8) Financing checklist: direct DSCR vs bridge-to-DSCR

### Direct DSCR may fit when
- property is already guest-ready
- STR revenue is supportable now
- repairs are mostly light cleanup / furnishing / merchandising
- lender accepts STR underwriting at needed leverage

### Bridge then DSCR may fit when
- value-add is needed to create second-key or added-bedroom thesis
- property needs capex before it can support target ADR
- porch enclosure / suite separation / light rehab is part of the plan
- refinance after stabilization improves hold economics

### Always verify
- rate
- LTV / LTC
- minimum DSCR
- prepay
- reserves
- STR eligibility
- seasoning / cash-out / refi rules

---

## 9) Deal killer checklist

Pass or materially reprice if any of these fail:
- wrong jurisdiction assumption
- no viable STR path for intended use
- septic cannot support bedroom/use plan
- access/ROW issue blocks intended operation
- floodplain/development issue makes expansion impractical
- guest suite is not operationally distinct enough to support second-key pricing
- appraisal treatment of added area is weak
- DSCR only works under heroic ADR assumptions

---

## 10) Minimum workflow for every new Fredericksburg/Gillespie deal

1. classify parcel: city vs ETJ vs county-only
2. classify HOT burden
3. verify STR permit burden
4. verify septic / utility / access / floodplain risks
5. verify second-key or expansion thesis
6. build comp stack
7. underwrite wholesale / flip / LTR / STR
8. run DSCR and bridge-to-DSCR options
9. identify appraisal-risk items
10. issue recommendation: pass / pursue / offer

- **City limit** = permit + annual inspection + city/state HOT
- **ETJ** = currently no city STR permit per city guidance, but still city/state HOT
- **County outside city/ETJ** = no countywide STR permit page found, but county/state HOT applies
