08 / Risks and Caveats

What Can Break the Thesis

This deal cannot be upgraded on narrative alone. The unresolved items are structural, not cosmetic.

Primary Risk Set

  • Portal blocking prevented a clean, public sold-comp package for same-street, neighborhood, and broader radius support.
  • The STR case depends on legality in the ETJ / county for both the main house and the guest suite.
  • Separate-rental viability of the guest suite is not verified by the brief.
  • Septic, parking, and utility capacity for dual-accommodation use are unverified.

Reconciliation Items

  • Title, GIS, MLS, and CAD should be reconciled before any offer decision.
  • Property identity and improvement set need confirmation against the listing narrative.
  • Neighborhood-bucket evidence was limited by rural inventory sparsity and portal access issues.
  • Loan terms and insurance are modeled assumptions, not lender-issued confirmations.
Important Caveat

This package is intentionally data-first and conservative. Where support is inferential, it is labeled as such. No recommendation here should be read as a substitute for verified county, title, lender, septic, parking, utility, or STR-permit diligence.

09 / Final Recommendation

Disposition Decision

The investment committee answer is straightforward at the current ask. Only one narrow path remains open, and it is contingent on diligence plus basis discipline.
PASS at Current Ask
Do not advance this asset at $525,000 as a wholesale, flip, or LTR acquisition.

The brief supports a clear pass at the current list price. Wholesale does not work, flip economics are not safe at list, and pure LTR debt coverage fails across the modeled purchase points.

The only potential re-entry path is a hybrid STR thesis that assumes both the main house and the guest suite can be rented legally and functionally. Even then, the package conclusion requires a materially lower basis, ideally into the low-to-mid $400s depending on diligence findings.
